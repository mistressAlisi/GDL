Responsible Gaming

Your well-being is our priority. We're committed to providing a safe and enjoyable gaming experience for all our players.

Gaming Guidelines

Set Time Limits

Play for entertainment, not as a way to make money. Set time limits for your gaming sessions and take regular breaks.

Budget Management

Only play with money you can afford to lose. Set deposit limits and stick to your budget.

Balance is Key

Gaming should be one of many activities you enjoy. Maintain a healthy balance with other aspects of your life.

Know the Signs

Be aware of problem gambling warning signs. If gaming stops being fun, it's time to take a break.

Warning Signs of Problem Gambling

Spending more money or time on gaming than you can afford

Having arguments with family or friends about your gaming habits

Chasing losses or trying to win back money you've lost

Neglecting work, school, or family obligations to play

Feeling anxious, depressed, or irritable when not playing

Borrowing money or selling possessions to fund gaming

Self-Exclusion & Control Tools

We offer various tools to help you maintain control over your gaming activities:

Deposit Limits

Set daily, weekly, or monthly deposit limits on your account.

Time-Out Periods

Take a break from gaming for 24 hours, 7 days, or 30 days.

Self-Exclusion

Temporarily or permanently exclude yourself from all gaming activities.

Need Help?

If you or someone you know is struggling with problem gambling, help is available 24/7:

National Problem Gambling Helpline

1-800-522-4700

National Council on Problem Gambling

www.ncpgambling.org

Gamblers Anonymous

www.gamblersanonymous.org

You must be 18 years or older to participate. Gaming should be entertaining, not a source of income.
